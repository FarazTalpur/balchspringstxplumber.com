Understanding Slab Leaks

Slab leaks occur when the pipes beneath your concrete foundation leak due to corrosion, pressure, or poor installation. These leaks can manifest as:

  • Unexplained water bill increases
  • Warm spots on floors
  • Cracks in the foundation
  • Mold growth and moisture accumulation

Ignoring these signs can lead to substantial damage, including mold infestations and foundation problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a slab leak include unexplained increases in water bills, damp or wet spots on floors, a decrease in water pressure, mold or mildew under carpets, and warm spots on the floor.

Slab leaks can be detected using specialized equipment such as electronic listening devices, infrared thermography, or using a pressure test to pinpoint the leak's location.

Slab leaks can be caused by a variety of factors, including pipe corrosion, abrasion from gravel or foundation movement, high water pressure, or poor construction quality during installation.

Yes, if left unaddressed, a slab leak can lead to significant foundation damage, including cracks in the foundation, structural instability, and an increased risk of further leaks.

Slab leaks can be repaired using several methods, including spot repairs where the leak is directly fixed, re-piping to bypass the leaking section, or using epoxy pipe coatings to seal the leak internally.

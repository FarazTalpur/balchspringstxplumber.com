Frequently Asked Questions

Bathroom drains often clog due to a buildup of hair, soap scum, toothpaste, and dirt. Over time, these substances can accumulate and form a blockage.

Regularly clean the drain cover and use a hair catcher. Avoid pouring excessive soap or toothpaste down the sink, and run hot water down the drain weekly to help break down any forming clogs.

You can try using a mixture of baking soda and vinegar, followed by hot water. Plungers can also help dislodge clogs, or you can use a drain snake to manually remove blockages.

If DIY methods fail, the clog keeps returning, or there are multiple drains in the home that are slow, it's time to call a professional plumber to assess and fix the issue.

Chemical drain cleaners can be effective but may damage pipes over time and pose health risks. They should be used sparingly and according to the instructions, or consider alternative methods for safety.

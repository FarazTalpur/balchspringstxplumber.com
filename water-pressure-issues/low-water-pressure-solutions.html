Common Causes of Low Water Pressure

Understanding the root causes of low water pressure is essential in finding the best remedy. Here are some typical culprits:

  • Pipe Leaks: Hidden leaks in your plumbing can significantly reduce water pressure.
  • Clogged Pipes: Mineral buildup or debris in pipes can obstruct water flow.
  • Faulty Pressure Regulator: A malfunctioning regulator might prevent adequate water pressure.
  • City Supply Issues: Sometimes, the problem originates from the municipal water supply.

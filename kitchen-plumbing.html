Frequently Asked Questions

You can unclog your kitchen sink by using a plunger, a plumbing snake, or a mixture of baking soda and vinegar. Pour the mixture down the drain, let it sit for 15-30 minutes, then flush with hot water. If the clog persists, consider contacting a professional plumber.

Low water pressure in a kitchen faucet can be caused by a clogged aerator, a blocked or leaking pipe, or problems with the water supply line. Cleaning the aerator and checking for any visible leaks can help diagnose the issue.

To fix a leaky kitchen faucet, first turn off the water supply. Remove the faucet handle, then replace the worn-out washer or O-ring inside the faucet. Reassemble the parts and turn the water back on to test for leaks.

If your garbage disposal is jammed, first turn off the power at the circuit breaker to avoid injury. Use an allen wrench to manually turn the blades from beneath the disposal. Remove any obstructions and reset the disposal using the reset button located on the bottom.

It's recommended to clean your kitchen drain once a month to prevent clogs and maintain proper function. Regularly use a mixture of vinegar and baking soda followed by hot water to keep it clear of buildup.

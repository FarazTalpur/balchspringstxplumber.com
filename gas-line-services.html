Frequently Asked Questions

Signs of a gas leak include a rotten egg smell, hissing sounds near gas lines, dying vegetation, or bubbles in standing water near the pipes. If you suspect a gas leak, evacuate immediately and contact your gas company or emergency services.

If you smell gas, do not use electrical switches or devices. Evacuate the area immediately, avoid open flames, and from a safe location, call your gas company or emergency services for assistance.

It's recommended to have your gas lines inspected annually by a licensed professional to ensure safety and proper function. Regular maintenance can help identify potential issues before they become serious problems.

It is highly recommended to have gas appliances installed by a licensed professional. Improper installation can lead to gas leaks or inefficient operation of the appliance, posing safety risks.

Regular gas line maintenance can prevent gas leaks, ensure efficient operation of appliances, and extend the lifespan of the gas system. It also enhances safety by identifying potential issues early on.
